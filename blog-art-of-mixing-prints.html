ART OF MIXING PRINTS AND PATTERNS LIKE A PRO

16 October 2020
by Farirai Macheka
  • Edgars
  • Summer
  • Wardrobe

We’ve all been there. You bump into a by-product of print mixing and before you know it, it looks like a happy uncoordinated mess. The fashion fiends never get tired of experimenting and pushing boundaries. Print-mixing is a trend that has been making its way into mainstream fashion and you really need to hop onto this bandwagon. When mixing prints and patterns together, and the end result is FABULOUS. If you are wondering how to pull this look, we got you covered

Neutral Colours

With so many prints to choose from, it's time to be daring and try this 70s trend. Before you jump onto this trend, however, we will cover the bases. With exciting prints and patterns there are neutral prints that you need to stock up on and these are


  1. Polka Dots
  2. Horizontal Stripes
  3. Gingham

Now you can check out chic ways to mix prints:

Similar Prints, Different Scale patterns

Let's get you set for summer. The easiest way to mix prints is to pair similar print with patterns of different colour scales. Small patterned black and white polka dot crop tops, large scale white and black polka dot maxi skirts and varying print scales keep the outfit interesting but not busy. These prints are supposed to be from the same family. To nail this look you need to be daring and ensure that your accessories such as shoes or handbags are a solid colour. We are thinking red heels or yellow flat thong sandals.

Different Prints

Gingham pants with polka dot statement sleeved top? Striped top with floral skater skirt? You are already thinking there is no way you are going to pull it off? It sounds unimaginable but fashion is about breaking the rules and owning the style with confidence. When going for this look, think colour. The key to print clashing (or avoiding it) is to make sure that there are same colours. In short there is one dominant colour from each piece. The colours should complement each other. Don't shy away from mixing different prints.

Accessorise

Step up your print mixing game and try completing the look with bold accessories. In case you may be wondering, accessories we mean belts, scarfs, shoes, handbags, hats etc. Once you start print mixing there is no holding back. One piece of your outfit either skirt or pants can be patterned. Here we are thinking plaid and the rest in solid colour. Then to bring your print clashing game a notch higher, go ahead and pair your plaid pants/ skirt with a plaid handbag in a different colour.

Break with a solid color

Another fool proof way of print mixing is layering on top with a solid jacket, blazer or duster. Incorporate different prints on your top or bottoms. You create a totally winning look. It's all about creating an illusion, making your mind adapt to the fact that you are print mixing and also gives a cohesive look while breaking the patterns.

Have fun and express your creativity. Print mixing is all about stepping out of your comfort zone. Done right you will have a chic fashion- forward vibe that's all centered on personality. Let the print-mixing do the talking. With these tips and tricks you are ready to print-mix like a pro. You are welcome!
